Myth 1:Chameleons change their skin colour in order to camouflage and hide from predators.

Truth 1: Chameleons actually change their colour to reflect the temperature and communicate with other chameleons, for example a female might switch to darker, more aggressive colours like purple, when she does not want to mate.


Myth 2: Sharks don't get cancer.

Truth 2: Now, you may be thinking, What's the big deal? but, this is an extremely strange, but popular belief. For years scientists have been studying cancerous tumors found within possession of sharks and surprising, they found that sharks' cells are not that different to our own and so sharks can have cancer.


Myth 3: An earthworm will regenerate into two when you cut it in half.

Truth 3: You may have been told as a child that when bisected, worms regenerate into two. But if you've ever experimented, then you were probably disappointed to still find two pieces of a single, dead worm (most likely because as a kid you sliced the worm's vital organs). Despite the fact that these worms do regenerate, it's only when a worm's head is decapitated from its tail. In this case, the head then manages to grow a new tail and the original tail simply dies.



Myth 4: Dogs sweat through their tongue.

Truth 4: This myth has stemmed from the fact that dogs start panting when they get tired. However, the 'catch' is that panting is not the same as sweating. Dogs have very few sweat glands, which are placed in their footpads, meaning that dogs definitely don't sweat through their tongue.


Myth 5: Bananas grow on trees.

Truth 5: Although it's called a 'banana tree', those plants that grow bananas don't actually have any woody tissue within it. In reality, it's a herbaceous plant, or herb.








Myth 6: Chloroform immediately knocks out people for several hours.

Truth 6: This is a popular belief fed to us by unrealistic films. It can actually take up to five minutes for someone to feel the effects of chloroform and even then, it only lasts for a short while.
