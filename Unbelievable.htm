Myth 1: If you eat less, you will lose weight.

Truth 1: Only a person who eats 5 or 6 meals a day will lose any weight.





Myth 2: Lightening never strikes the same place twice.

Truth 2: This popular myth was found to be wrong when, during a particularly rough storm, the Empire State Building was struck by lightening 8 times, within only 24 minutes.


Myth 3: Neanderthals were a less evolved ancestor of humans.

Truth 3: In reality, Neanderthals and humans were actually two different species that lived at the same time, in different parts of the globe. And it was the fact that humans were smarter and stronger that enabled them to survive for longer than Neanderthals.


Myth 4: The Great Wall of China is the only man-made structure visible from space.

Truth 4: This is one of the most misleading myths ever! It was proven by astronauts that this iconic wall cannot be seen from space, only at extremely low altitudes. It was also found that roads and plane runways are easier to see from space than the wall itself!


Myth 5: You must wait 24 hours before filing a 'Missing Persons Report'

Truth 5: It's a common belief that people hold, stemmed from one too many crime shows. In the real world, police don't actually require a 24 hour waiting period before accepting missing-persons reports.




Myth 6: Bulls become aggravated when you show them the colour red

Truth 6: Bulls are actually colour blind. So there's no way the colour red is capable of provoking bulls into coming after you. Bull's go after bullfighters because it's the motion of moving the cloth that they perceive as a threat.
